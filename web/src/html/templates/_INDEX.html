Welcome

This site is a tool to track seed collections and to keep records about how those seeds have been used. It is geared toward native wild species rather than cultivated vegetable and flower varieties.

There are three basic concepts to explain: Samples, Collections, and Locations.

Samples refer to a single collection event for a particular species at a particular location. So if you went to a friend's property (with permission, no doubt) and collected a bag of little bluestem seeds, and a bag of skyblue aster seeds, that would be represented as two samples. If you went back and collected more little bluestem seeds next year, that would be another new sample.

Locations refer to the place where the seed was collected. Than be well defined, or it can be vague. You're free to interpret it how you wish. In the previous example, "your friend's property" would be the location. This could also be something like a seed seller where you purchased the seeds.

Collections are sets of seed samples grouped for a specific purpose. For example, if you were working on a restoration project and wanted to keep track of what you planted in a particular location, you could create a collection for that project and add the appropriate seed samples to that collection.
